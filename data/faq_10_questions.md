# 高壓滅菌鍋 FAQ（10 個問題）

## Q1: 高壓滅菌的溫度和時間需求是什麼？

**A:** 在進行高壓滅菌時，溫度和時間的設定是確保滅菌效果的關鍵。一般而言，高壓滅菌的標準條件為121攝氏度持續15至20分鐘。此設定主要針對大多數實驗室器材和醫療用品，能有效殺滅細菌、病毒和孢子等微生物。為了達到最佳效果，滅菌物品的包裝和擺放必須允許蒸汽充分接觸。

在某些情況下，例如滅菌較大的物品或特殊材料，可能需要提高溫度至134攝氏度並縮短滅菌時間至3至5分鐘。這種高溫短時間的設定通常適用於耐高溫材料。

高壓滅菌設備的操作應遵循相關標準和規範，例如ISO 17665和EN 285，這些標準詳述了高壓滅菌過程的性能要求和驗證方法。此外，定期校準及維護設備也是不可忽視的，確保設備在最佳狀態運行，避免滅菌失效。

在實際操作中，使用者應根據物品的材質和滅菌需求選擇合適的程序，並在每次滅菌後使用化學或生物指示劑進行效果驗證，確保滅菌過程的有效性。這樣不僅能達到所需的衛生標準，還能延長設備和物品的使用壽命。

## Q2: 高壓蒸氣滅菌保存期限？

**A:** 高壓蒸氣滅菌後的物品保存期限主要取決於滅菌包裝的完整性、儲存環境以及使用的包材類型。一般而言，若包裝材料完好且存放於乾燥、清潔、無塵的環境下，滅菌後的物品可以保存約6個月至1年。然而，具體保存期限可能因不同的標準而有所差異。

依據美國疾病控制與預防中心（CDC）和美國食品藥品監督管理局（FDA）的建議，應定期檢查滅菌包裝是否受到污染或損壞，這樣可防止滅菌效力的喪失。若包裝破損或受到污染，則應重新進行滅菌。

此外，使用無紡布、塑膠袋或防潮紙等材料進行包裝，可以有效延長保存期限。這些材料具備防潮、防塵的特性，能維持包裝的完整性。值得注意的是，醫療機構應遵循現行的國際標準，如ISO 11607，確保包裝材料和過程符合規範要求。

為了確保滅菌效果持續有效，建立一套良好的庫存管理制度也是必要的。這包括標示滅菌日期及失效日期，並採用「先進先出」的原則，以確保使用的物品在有效期限內。這些措施能確保高壓蒸氣滅菌物品的安全性及有效性。

## Q3: How to choose an autoclave?

**A:** Choosing an autoclave involves several key considerations to ensure it meets your specific needs and complies with relevant standards. First, determine the type and size based on your operational requirements. Laboratory autoclaves are typically benchtop or floor-standing, while industrial applications may require larger, horizontal units. Consider the volume and types of materials you will be sterilizing, such as glassware, media, or instruments, to decide on the appropriate chamber size.

Next, evaluate the control systems and features. Look for models with programmable cycles, pre-vacuum and post-vacuum options, and automatic drying functions, which enhance efficiency and sterilization effectiveness. Some advanced autoclaves offer data logging and remote monitoring, which can be useful for compliance and quality control purposes.

Ensure the autoclave complies with relevant standards. In Taiwan, compliance with international standards like ISO 13485 for medical devices or EN 285 for large steam sterilizers is crucial. These standards ensure the reliability and safety of the equipment. Additionally, if the autoclave is used for processing materials in a regulated environment, it should meet FDA guidelines where applicable.

Consider the maintenance and support services offered by the manufacturer, as regular maintenance is vital for the longevity and performance of the autoclave. Check for local service availability and the warranty terms.

Finally, budget is an important factor. While initial costs are significant, consider the long-term benefits and operational costs, including energy consumption and maintenance, to make a cost-effective decision. By carefully evaluating these factors, you can select an autoclave that meets your operational needs and regulatory requirements.

## Q4: What are the factors of autoclave?

**A:** When considering the factors that influence the effectiveness and efficiency of an autoclave, several key elements should be taken into account. First, temperature and pressure settings are critical. Typically, autoclaves operate at temperatures ranging from 121°C to 134°C with pressures of approximately 15 to 30 psi. These parameters ensure the effective sterilization of equipment by killing microorganisms and spores. It's essential to follow standards such as ISO 17665, which provides guidelines for steam sterilization processes.

The type and load of material being sterilized also influence autoclave performance. For instance, porous loads, like textiles, require specific cycles to ensure steam penetrates thoroughly. In contrast, liquid loads need gradual cooling to prevent boiling over. Understanding the material's properties and selecting the appropriate cycle is crucial for effective sterilization.

Routine maintenance and validation are vital. Regular calibration and validation of the autoclave, as per standards like EN 285 for large steam sterilizers, ensure consistent performance. This includes checking the integrity of the autoclave chamber, door seal, and pressure gauges to prevent leaks and ensure safety.

Moreover, consider the water quality used in the autoclave. Using deionized or distilled water can prevent mineral build-up, which can affect the efficiency and longevity of the unit.

Lastly, user training cannot be overlooked. Proper training ensures that operators understand the specific requirements of different loads, the importance of not overloading, and the need for correct cycle selection. This helps in maintaining the integrity of the sterilization process and ensuring compliance with relevant health and safety regulations, such as those outlined by the FDA for medical devices.

## Q5: Which is better Class N or B autoclave?

**A:** When choosing between a Class N and a Class B autoclave, it’s important to consider the specific sterilization needs and regulatory requirements of your practice or laboratory. Class B autoclaves are generally more versatile and capable of sterilizing a wider range of instruments due to their advanced vacuum technology, which is compliant with European standard EN 13060. This type of autoclave is particularly effective for sterilizing hollow, wrapped, and porous loads, making it suitable for dental, medical, and laboratory settings that require rigorous sterilization of complex instruments.

Class N autoclaves, on the other hand, are typically less expensive and simpler in design. They are suitable for sterilizing solid, unwrapped instruments and are often used in settings where the sterilization of simple, non-porous loads is sufficient. These autoclaves rely on gravity displacement and do not feature vacuum cycles, which limits their ability to effectively sterilize wrapped or hollow items.

For facilities that need to comply with stringent sterilization standards, such as those outlined by ISO 17665 or FDA guidelines for healthcare settings, a Class B autoclave is recommended. This ensures compliance with high-level sterilization requirements, reducing the risk of infection and ensuring patient safety. It is advisable to assess the types of instruments used and the regulatory environment before making a decision, as selecting an autoclave that meets all operational needs is crucial for effective sterilization.

## Q6: What are the steps to use an autoclave?

**A:** To use an autoclave effectively and safely, follow these essential steps:

1. **Preparation**: Begin by checking that the autoclave is clean and in good working condition. Ensure the water reservoir is filled with distilled water to prevent mineral buildup. Gather all items to be sterilized and ensure they are compatible with steam sterilization (e.g., metals, glassware, certain plastics).

2. **Loading**: Properly load the autoclave without overcrowding. Items should be arranged to allow steam penetration; for example, position containers with lids slightly ajar. Use autoclave bags for loose instruments or materials, and ensure they are not sealed airtight.

3. **Setting Parameters**: Close the autoclave door securely. Select the appropriate cycle based on the items being sterilized, typically involving temperature settings around 121-134°C and pressure settings of 15-30 psi. Standard cycles often last 15-30 minutes, but this can vary based on load size and material.

4. **Operation**: Start the autoclave. The machine will begin by purging air, then pressurizing and heating to the selected parameters. Monitors and gauges should be observed to ensure the autoclave reaches and maintains the correct conditions throughout the cycle.

5. **Cooling and Unloading**: Once the cycle completes, allow the autoclave to depressurize and cool before opening the door. This prevents burns and ensures the effective drying of items. Carefully unload items, using heat-resistant gloves if necessary.

6. **Record Keeping**: Document each cycle's parameters and results, as required by standards like ISO 17665 or FDA guidelines. This ensures compliance and traceability.

7. **Maintenance**: Regularly maintain the autoclave according to the manufacturer’s instructions, including routine inspections, cleaning, and calibration checks to ensure optimal performance and compliance with safety standards.

These steps ensure effective sterilization, while adherence to specific guidelines and standards ensures both safety and regulatory compliance.

## Q7: How often should the autoclave be cleaned and maintained?

**A:** Autoclaves should be cleaned and maintained regularly to ensure their optimal performance and compliance with health and safety standards. Generally, daily, weekly, and periodic maintenance routines are recommended.

**Daily Maintenance:**
- Inspect the autoclave for any visible signs of damage or leaks. Clean the door gasket with a damp cloth to ensure a proper seal during operation.
- Remove any debris or residue from inside the chamber and trays, as buildup can affect sterilization efficacy.
- Check the water level, if applicable, and ensure that the water used meets the manufacturer's specifications, often distilled or deionized water.

**Weekly Maintenance:**
- Conduct a more thorough cleaning of the chamber and external surfaces using a mild detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals that could damage the autoclave materials.
- Inspect and clean the drain line to prevent clogs, which can impact pressure and temperature control.

**Periodic Maintenance:**
- Depending on usage frequency, perform a more detailed inspection and maintenance every 3 to 6 months. This might include checking the pressure relief valve, calibrating the temperature and pressure controls, and ensuring that all safety features are functioning correctly.
- Replace any worn parts, such as door gaskets or filters, as recommended by the manufacturer.

Adhering to specific standards like ISO 17665, which provides guidelines for sterilization processes, can also help ensure compliance and efficacy. Additionally, follow any specific recommendations from the autoclave manufacturer, as these can vary based on the model and design.

Regular maintenance not only extends the lifespan of the autoclave but also ensures that it operates safely and effectively, which is critical for meeting health and safety regulations.

## Q8: How to troubleshoot an autoclave?

**A:** When troubleshooting an autoclave, start by identifying the nature of the problem, such as temperature, pressure, or mechanical issues. Here’s a structured approach:

1. **Check Power Supply and Connections**: Ensure that the autoclave is properly connected to a power source and that circuit breakers are not tripped. Inspect all cables and connections for signs of wear or damage.

2. **Inspect Door Seal and Locking Mechanism**: A common issue is the failure of the door seal, which can prevent the autoclave from reaching the necessary pressure. Examine the gasket for wear or debris and ensure the door locks securely.

3. **Review Temperature and Pressure Readings**: Verify that the autoclave reaches the required temperature and pressure levels. If not, check the steam generation system, including the boiler and valves. Calibration of sensors may be necessary, following standards such as ISO 17665 for sterilization processes.

4. **Examine Water and Steam Quality**: Poor water or steam quality can affect performance. Ensure water supply is adequate and free from impurities. Regularly clean and maintain the steam generator and filters.

5. **Evaluate Cycle Parameters**: Confirm that the correct cycle is selected for the load type. Misconfigured cycles may result in incomplete sterilization.

6. **Maintenance and Calibration**: Regular maintenance in accordance with the manufacturer’s guidelines and applicable standards (e.g., ISO 13485 for medical devices) is crucial. This includes lubrication, part replacement, and calibration.

7. **Error Codes and Diagnostic Tools**: Utilize the autoclave’s diagnostic features to identify error codes. Refer to the user manual for specific troubleshooting steps related to these codes.

8. **Consult the Manufacturer**: If issues persist, contact the manufacturer for technical support or arrange for professional servicing, ensuring compliance with local regulations and standards such as EN 285 for large steam sterilizers.

By methodically addressing these areas, many common issues can be resolved, ensuring the autoclave operates efficiently and safely.

## Q9: What are the safety precautions when handling an autoclave?

**A:** When handling an autoclave, several critical safety precautions must be observed to ensure both personal safety and the integrity of the sterilization process. First, always wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE), including heat-resistant gloves, lab coats, and face shields, to protect against high temperatures and steam burns. Before using the autoclave, thoroughly inspect it for any signs of damage or malfunction, such as gasket wear or door misalignment, as these could lead to safety hazards.

Ensure that all materials to be autoclaved are compatible with high-pressure steam. Avoid sealing containers completely, as pressure buildup can lead to explosions. Instead, use vented caps or cover them loosely with aluminum foil. Proper loading is crucial; avoid overloading the chamber to allow steam to circulate freely around the items. Follow the manufacturer’s guidelines for load size and arrangement.

During operation, never attempt to open the autoclave door until the pressure gauge reads zero and the temperature has dropped to a safe level. Most modern autoclaves include safety interlocks that prevent opening under pressure, but manual verification is always prudent. After the cycle, open the door slightly to allow steam to clear before fully opening it.

Regular maintenance and calibration of the autoclave are vital for safe operation. Refer to relevant standards such as ISO 17665 for sterilization processes and ensure routine inspections and servicing by qualified personnel. Proper documentation of maintenance and cycle parameters should be maintained to comply with quality assurance protocols.

In case of an emergency, be familiar with the location and use of safety equipment, such as emergency shut-off valves and eyewash stations. Conduct regular training sessions on autoclave safety procedures to ensure all operators are well-informed about potential hazards and the correct response actions.

## Q10: What cannot be sterilized in an autoclave?

**A:** Certain materials and items are not suitable for sterilization in an autoclave due to their physical or chemical properties. Autoclaves use high-pressure steam at temperatures typically ranging from 121°C to 134°C to achieve sterilization, which can damage incompatible materials.

1. **Heat-sensitive materials**: Items such as certain plastics, some types of rubber, and delicate electronics cannot withstand autoclave conditions and may melt or deform. Items made from materials like polystyrene, polyethylene, and polypropylene often fall into this category unless specifically designed for autoclaving.

2. **Volatile or flammable substances**: Chemicals with low boiling points or those that are flammable should not be autoclaved. The high heat and pressure can cause these substances to vaporize or ignite, posing safety hazards.

3. **Corrosive substances**: Strong acids, bases, and other corrosive chemicals can damage the autoclave’s components, particularly those made of metal. These substances should be sterilized using alternative methods.

4. **Certain biological materials**: Materials that can be damaged by moisture, such as certain types of powders, may clump or lose efficacy when exposed to steam. Additionally, items that contain oils or greases are not suitable for steam sterilization as they can hinder effective heat penetration.

5. **Radioactive materials**: These require specialized handling and should not be placed in standard autoclaves due to contamination risks and regulatory issues.

In practice, consult relevant standards such as ISO 17665 for moist heat sterilization and follow manufacturer guidelines to ensure compatibility of materials with autoclave processes. Proper assessment and testing should be conducted when in doubt to avoid damage and ensure effective sterilization.
